Artwork

Some of my favorite things I’ve made over the years

Frames, 2024

Inspired by a couple of poster frames and a mirror I found near a dumpster, this piece allows me to display some of my favorite photos.

Big Triangle, 2023

When I was in middle school, I doodled a half colored-in triangle on my wrist. Since then, I have made it a bit of a personal logo (see the icon for this website). I draw it on the back of all of my inline skates, and wanted to display it in a bigger way. This wall piece is me trying to do that. I started with a cardboard silhouette and gradually built the actual piece on top of it. It took me about a year to complete, and I ended up finishing it while recovering from a concussion.

Wall Wheels, 2021

I saved up the used wheels from my inline skates for a few years. Then I screwed them to a board on the wall.

Bone TV Stand, 2021

In my second year of college, I found some biomechanically accurate artificial left femurs in one of the tech recycling bins. Naturally, I gathered up as many as I could carry and took them home. They became decorations in every bedroom I had for the rest of college. When I finished school, it was time for me to get my own TV, and I needed a stand. Now the bones have a new life holding up my TV.

Run the Jewels Hands, 2020

This piece is a recreation of the album artwork of hip hop duo, Run the Jewels (specifically their first two albums). I worked on the hands between semesters at school starting with wire “skeletons” in December 2019 and completing them in July 2020. Other than the album artwork, I also used my own hands as reference for getting the shapes and scaling correct.

College Shadowbox, 2020

I found an old industrial computer monitor, and filled it with significant items from my undergraduate experience. Also, I made it light up.

Freeline Skate Holder, 2018

Just a little box that I decorated and made able to hold my Freeline skates.

Danger Donut, 2017

I made this one in part to impress somebody I had a crush on at the time. I’m not sure if it worked or not. People often seem appalled by the fact that I have broken glass out in the open, but nobody has been hurt by it yet.

Skate Frame Lamp, 2017

In my sophomore year of college, the dorm room I was staying in didn’t come with any lights, so I had to bring my own lamp. I took the frames from my old inline skates, and turned them into this.
